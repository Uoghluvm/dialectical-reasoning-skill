# Worked Example: 有计划的市场经济

Use this file when the user asks why `有计划的市场经济` is not just a stitched-together contradiction in terms.

## Starting Point

The shallow reading says `计划` and `市场` are mutually exclusive:

- plan means centralized direction
- market means decentralized exchange

That is a metaphysical framing because it treats each term as fixed and self-contained.

## Contradiction Map

A more useful dialectical map is:

- long-cycle coordination vs short-cycle price discovery
- public goals vs private incentives
- macro stability vs micro flexibility
- strategic capacity building vs immediate allocative efficiency

These are real tensions, but they are not simple either-or choices.

## Principal Contradiction at Different Stages

The principal contradiction changes with development stage.

Examples:

- In early industrial catch-up, the principal contradiction may be between weak national productive capacity and the need for accelerated coordination. Planning tends to be the principal aspect.
- In a more differentiated economy, the principal contradiction may shift toward rigidity, information bottlenecks, or innovation drag. Market mechanisms gain weight as the principal aspect in many domains.

The key point is that the relation between plan and market is staged, conditional, and domain-specific.

## Unity of Opposites

Planning and market are not only opponents.

- Planning without market feedback can become blind.
- Market without planning can underprovide public goods, long-term infrastructure, industrial policy, and resilience.
- Market signals can be used inside planned strategic direction.
- Planning can set boundaries, priorities, and public objectives without replacing every exchange relation.

The contradiction therefore pushes toward mediated forms, not pure poles.

## Quantitative to Qualitative Change

When marketization expands beyond certain thresholds without public coordination, qualitative problems may appear:

- infrastructure gaps
- regional imbalance
- fragile supply chains
- short-termism

When planning expands beyond its own thresholds, different qualitative problems appear:

- information overload
- bureaucratic rigidity
- weak experimentation
- slow adaptation

Dialectical analysis asks where the threshold lies for the concrete system, not which slogan sounds cleaner.

## Dialectical Synthesis

A dialectical synthesis is not half-planning plus half-market by arithmetic.

It usually looks like:

- strategic planning at the macro and long-cycle level
- market exchange and experimentation at the micro and local level
- public regulation to stop destructive spillovers
- periodic adjustment as the principal contradiction changes

So `有计划的市场经济` can be coherent when plan and market are treated as historically mediated functions inside a developing system rather than as timeless absolute categories.

## Reusable Output Pattern

When answering this topic, prefer:

1. explain why the contradiction is real
2. explain why the contradiction is also productive
3. show the stage-specific principal aspect
4. identify the threshold where one side becomes destructive
5. conclude with a mediated institutional form
