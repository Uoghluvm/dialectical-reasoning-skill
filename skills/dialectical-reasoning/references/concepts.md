# Core Concepts

Use this file when the user needs a concise conceptual refresher before applying the skill.

## Contradiction

Treat contradiction as an internal relation between forces, tendencies, or requirements that both oppose and presuppose each other. A contradiction is not just disagreement. It shapes how the object moves.

Ask:

- What two or more forces are pulling the object in different directions?
- Why can neither side simply be removed without changing the object itself?
- What practical form does the contradiction take right now?

## Principal Contradiction

Find the contradiction that most strongly organizes the situation at the current stage. Do not assume it is permanent.

Ask:

- Which contradiction currently decides the main direction of development?
- Which contradiction, if shifted, would reconfigure the rest?
- What evidence says this is principal now rather than historically or abstractly?

## Principal Aspect

Within a contradiction, identify which side currently leads, dominates, or sets the practical limits. This can reverse over time.

Ask:

- Which side currently shapes the terms of motion?
- Which side is constrained but still strategically important?
- What developments could reverse the relation?

## Unity of Opposites

Show how the opposing sides depend on one another even while they struggle. Avoid presenting contradiction as pure external collision.

Examples:

- Competition may generate innovation while depending on shared legal and material infrastructure.
- Planning may discipline long cycles while depending on market signals for local adaptation.

## Quantitative Change and Qualitative Change

Track accumulation, thresholds, and phase shifts. Small shifts often matter only after crossing a threshold.

Ask:

- What is gradually accumulating?
- Where is the threshold?
- What new quality appears once the threshold is crossed?

## Mediation and Conditions

Always specify the mediating institutions, technologies, incentives, and historical conditions that make a contradiction concrete. Abstract dialectics without mediation becomes slogan production.

## Practical Heuristic

When the analysis feels vague, rebuild it with this sequence:

1. Name the object.
2. Name the contradiction.
3. Name the principal contradiction.
4. Name the principal aspect now.
5. Name the threshold or turning point.
6. Name the condition that would reverse the judgment.
